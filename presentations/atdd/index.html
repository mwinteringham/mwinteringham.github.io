What's acceptable?

  • The goal of a software development team is to deliver something our business or customer desires
  • Failing to do so will result in our product or project failing
  • So how do we know what to deliver to our business or customer. How do we know what is acceptable to them
  • I'm going to show two screenshots of the same web page in different states and I want to know which is acceptable
  • Flipchart how many put their hand up for each without CSS
  • Flipchart how many put their hand up for each with CSS
  • Ask why people think one is more acceptable than the other. Do both

  • 2. A tool doesn't consider the assumptions and emotions of the business / end user / people that matter

The automated acceptance testing paradox

Mind map different risks around a requirement. Start with the initial risk that we deliver what the business wants

Automated Acceptance tests are for guiding not validating

'A failed check is an invitation to explore'

  • First of all, for me, things that are automated are checks and we use them to help monitor change NOT validate the application
  • That helps me understand their role as guides in my testing
  • If an acceptance check fails then I need to explore that feature
  • If we don't question failing or passing acceptance checks, there are things we don't about what's been delivered
  • This means I will probably need to engage with the business or a rep of the business
  • Acceptance checks as guides can be a heuristic to set me on a path to ask the right questions

Wrapping up

  • Be aware of your assumptions
  • Use automated acceptance checks as guides
  • Other testing activities are required
